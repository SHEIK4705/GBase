What is Pronoun

A pronoun is a word we use instead of repeating a noun. It helps make sentences shorter and clearer.

For Example :
Instead of saying "Mary is my friend. Mary likes to read books," you can say "Mary is my friend. She likes to read books," using "she" as a pronoun for Mary.

Types of Pronoun


  • Subject Pronouns: Used as the subject of a sentence. Example: "I" am happy.
  • Object Pronouns: Used as the object of a verb or preposition. Example: John likes "me".
  • Possessive Pronouns: Show ownership or possession. Example: "Mine" is the red one.
  • Reflexive Pronouns: Reflect back to the subject. Example: I hurt "myself".
  • Demonstrative Pronouns: Point to specific things. Example: Look at "this" book.
  • Interrogative Pronouns: Used to ask questions. Example: "Who" is at the door?
  • Indefinite Pronouns: Refer to non-specific persons or things. Example: "Everyone" loves pizza.
  • Relative Pronouns: Introduce relative clauses. Example: The person who won the race is my friend.

Note:

Now we will see relative pronoun and possesive pronoun.

Relative Pronoun

Defn : These are used to introduce relative clauses, which provide additional information about a noun in the main clause.

Pronouns and Examples
  • Who: Used for people. Example: "The girl who is singing is my sister."
  • Whom: Also used for people, often as the object of a verb or preposition.
    Example: "The man whom I met is a doctor."
  • Which: Used for things or animals.
    Example: "The car which is parked outside is mine."
  • That: Used for things, animals, or people.
    Example: "The book that I'm reading is very interesting."
  • Whose: Shows possession and is used for people, things, or animals.
    Example: "The dog whose tail is wagging belongs to my neighbor."
  • Of which: Indicates possession and is used for things or animals.
    Example: "The house, of which the roof is red, is for sale."

Possessive Pronouns

Defn : A possessive pronoun is a type of pronoun that shows ownership or possession. Instead of using the noun directly, a possessive pronoun indicates that something belongs to someone or something.

Pronouns an Example
  1. Mine:
    This is my book. The book is mine.
  2. Yours:
    Is this your pen? Yes, it's yours.
  3. His:
    This is John's car. The car is his.
  4. Hers:
    That is Mary's phone. The phone is hers.
  5. Its:
    The cat is grooming its fur.
  6. Ours:
    We share the same house. The house is ours.
  7. Theirs:
    Those are their shoes. The shoes are theirs.

How to avoid mistakes

  • Agreement: Ensure pronouns match their antecedents in number, gender, and person.
  • Correct Case: Use the right pronoun case (subjective, objective, possessive).
  • Clarity: Avoid ambiguity in pronoun reference.
  • Consistency: Maintain uniform pronoun usage.
