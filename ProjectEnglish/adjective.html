What are Adjectives

Adjectives are words that describe things. They tell us more about nouns, like how they look, feel, or act.

Examples

  • Big elephant (describing size)
  • Red apple (describing color)
  • Happy child (describing emotion)

Different Types of Adjectives

1. Describing Words

These adjectives describe what something is like.

Example

  • Tall tree (describing height)
  • Funny joke (describing humor)
2. Pointing Words

These adjectives point out which thing we're talking about.

Example

  • This book (pointing to a specific book)
  • Those shoes (pointing to specific shoes)
3. Ownership Words

These adjectives show who owns something.

Example

  • My cat (showing possession)
  • Her house (showing possession)
4. Asking Words

These adjectives help ask questions about things.

  • Which one (asking about choice)
  • Whose bag (asking about ownership)

Making Things Bigger or Better


  1. To compare two things, add "-er" or use "more." Example :
    • Taller (comparing height)
    • More beautiful (comparing appearance)
  2. To compare three or more things, add "-est" or use "most." Example :
    • Tallest (comparing height)
    • Most beautiful (comparing appearance)

Tricky Adjectives to Remember

  1. Good vs. Well : Good describes things, while well describes actions. Example :
    • The cake tastes good. (describing the cake)
    • She dances well. (describing the action of dancing)
  2. Few vs. Less : Few is for counting things, while less is for things you can't count. Example :
    • I have few books. (countable)
    • I have less water. (uncountable)
How to Order Adjectives

When using more than one adjective, put them in this order :

  1. Opinion (like good or bad)
  2. Size (like big or small)
  3. Age (like old or young)
  4. Shape (like round or square)
  5. Color (like red or blue)
  6. Origin (like American or Italian)
  7. Material (like wooden or metal)
  8. Purpose (like cooking or dining)
Example for above
    beautiful (opinion) big (size) old (age) round (shape) red (color) italian (origin) wooden (material) dining (purpose) table

Examples

  • The big tree provided shade on a hot day.
  • We watched the bright stars in the night sky.
  • The old house creaked in the wind.
  • She received a surprise gift on her birthday.
  • They enjoyed a peaceful evening by the fireplace.

Real Life Example

  • Advertisement: New soft towels for sale!
  • Social Media Post: Had a fun day at the beach!
  • Restaurant Menu: Try our delicious pasta dishes!
  • Product Packaging: Get the fresh taste of our fruit juice.
  • Travel Brochure: Visit the beautiful beaches of Hawaii!
  • Movie Review: It's a scary movie with lots of twists.

Tips for Learning

  • Practice using adjectives in sentences.
  • Pay attention to the order of adjectives.
  • Read books or stories to find more adjectives.

Adjective Exercise


1. She has a cat that loves to cuddle.

2. The flowers bloomed in the garden.

3. He wore a suit to the job interview.

4. Diesel is (heavy) than petrol.

5. I bought a book at the bookstore yesterday.

6. Gold is (expansive) than silver.

7. A wise enemy is (good) than a foolish friend.


Identify the Adjectives

8. Mary had a little lamb.

9. The brave army marched through the streets.

10. I had no idea such a fantastic place existed.


Answers

  1. cute
  2. beautiful
  3. nice
  4. heavier
  5. new
  6. more expensive
  7. better
  8. little
  9. brave
  10. fantastic
