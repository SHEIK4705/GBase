Definition

Wh-type questions are questions that start with words like "who," "what," "where," "when," "why," "how," "which," "whom," or "whose." They are used to ask about different things, actions, places, times, reasons, methods, choices, people, or possessions.

Syntax

  • wh + an auxiliary verb + subject + main verb (do, be, have....) ?
  • wh + modal verb + subject + main verb ?
    •

Common Words

  1. Who:
    Use "who" to inquire about people or persons.
    Example: "Who is coming to the party?"
  2. Whom:
    Use "whom" as the object form of "who" in formal English to inquire about the object of an action.
    Example: "To whom did you give the book?"
  3. What:
    Use "what" to ask about things, actions, qualities, or ideas.
    Example: "What is your favorite food?"
  4. Where:
    Use "where" to ask about locations or places.
    Example: "Where did you go on vacation?"
  5. When:
    Use "when" to inquire about time or specific moments.
    Example: "When is your birthday?"
  6. Why:
    Use "why" to ask about reasons or explanations.
    Example: "Why did you choose this career path?"
  7. How:
    Use "how" to ask about manner, method, or condition.
    Example: "How did you learn to speak Spanish?"
  8. Which:
    Use "which" when selecting from a limited number of options or when asking about a specific choice.
    Example: "Which movie do you want to watch?"
  9. Whose:
    Use "whose" to inquire about possession or ownership.
    Example: "Whose car is parked outside?"

Examples

Change sentence to wh-type questions

  • Sentence: I go to Chennai.
    Ans : Where do you go?
  • Sentence: She eats lunch at noon.
    Ans : When does she eat lunch?
  • Sentence: They play soccer in the park.
    Ans : Where do they play soccer?
  • Sentence: He reads books in the library.
    Ans : Where does he read books?
  • Sentence: We watch movies on weekends.
    Ans : When do we watch movies?
  • Sentence: She listens to music in her room.
    Ans : Where does she listen to music?
Excercise

How to avoid mistakes

  1. Proofread: Check your work carefully before finishing to catch errors.
  2. Double-Check: Verify important details like dates and names to ensure accuracy.
  3. Learn from Mistakes: Understand errors to prevent repeating them in the future.
