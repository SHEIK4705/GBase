Simple Present

Format : Sub + verb (present)

I/ You/ We/ They = verb (present)

He/ She/ It = verb(s)

When we use
  • Habitual Action
  • Universal Truth
  • Expressing Opinion
  • Scheduled Events
Example
  • "She walks to work every morning." (habitual action)
  • "Water boils at 100 degrees Celsius." (general truth)
  • "The train leaves at 9 AM tomorrow." (scheduled event)
  • "I believe he is right." (expressing opinion)
  • "Close the door, please." (giving instructions)
Keywords

Important Keywords:
   Always, Often, Usually, Sometimes, Every, Never, At, In, Believe, Do

Present Continuous

Format : Sub + am/ is/ are + verb(ing)

I = am

You/ We/ They = are

He/ She/ It = is

When we use
  • Ongoing Action
  • Future Plans
  • Now (what's happening right now)
  • Temporary Action (Not habitual or permanent)
Examples
  • "I am eating dinner right now" (Action happening right now)
  • "We are going to the beach tomorrow" (Future Plans)
  • "She is studying for her exam at the moment" (Temporary Action)
  • "It is raining outside" (Ongoing action)
Keywords

Important Keywords:
   Now, At the moment, Currently, Today, Tonight, Tomorrow, Right now, while, Presently, Listen!, Look!

Present Perfect

Format : Sub + have/ has + verb (past participle - V3)

I/ You/ We/ They = have

He/ She/ It = has

When we use
  • An Action Just Completed
  • Talks about things that happened but don't specify when.
  • Shows actions that started in the past and still matter now.
  • Often used for experiences or important events.
Examples
  • She has visited Paris before. (Action occurred at an unspecified time in the past.)
  • They have lived here for five years. (Action began in the past and continues into the present.)
  • He has never seen that movie. (Action has not occurred at any time before now.)
Keywords

Important keywords :
   Already, Just, Just Now, So far, Not yet

Present Perfect Continuous

Format : Sub + have/ has + been + verb (ing) + O

I/ You/ We/ They = have

He/ She/ It = has

When we use
  • Present perfect continuous tense shows actions that started in the past and are still happening now.
  • We use it when we want to talk about how long something has been going on, or how much progress has been made.
Examples
  • "She has been working on her project all day." (Duration of action)
  • "We have been cleaning the house for hours." (Progress of action)
  • "She has been waiting for the bus since noon." (since example)
Keywords

Important Keywords :
   for, since, ....
